UCL PPLV Research Area


The UCL Research Team is leading new developments into this field.
Particularly of note, Peter O'Hearn of the UCL PPLV research team developed Separation Logic which as has facilitated the development of programs to verify programs written in imperative programmming languages. Members of the UCL PPLV research team were also directly involved in developing Facebook's INFER program, implementing separation logic which is used to ensure that phone applications can be developed quickly.

Research Projects and Impacts

Once again, for the sake of brevity we haven't been able to cover all the research projects undertaken by the PPLV department, so we've chosen to display a few that we found the most interesting.


Gaming Market ( Boosted automatic verification Research Project )

The core code (known as kernel code) for the Graphical Processing Unit (GPU), is responsible for tasks such as a high FPS rate, smooth gameplay and a good visual appearnce. This is now being developed by automatic verification techniques ( mainly algorithims - Sequence of instructions to ahcieve a certain goal ). The low level code required for the GPU to function is its kernel code - previously this was developed and tested by humans however in the 21st century it is developed using automatic verification algorithms. The algorithms find a way to maxmimise memory usage and identify bugs faster than any human could. As a result of these increases in efficiency, the UK has experienced economic growth in it's gaming industry as there is currently a high demand for GPUs.


Phone industry ( Program Verification Research Project )

In wider Society the use of boosted automatic verification by UCL is being studied based on the number of android phones in the current. There are over 250,000,000 of them in use, so using such as a technique will increase the productivity of finding crashes, bugs and memory problems.


Business Sector ( Algebra and Logic for Policy and Utility in Information Security Project )

By producing a universal way of representing security issues within a system, managers within the industry now have a basis on how to model their policies to be sustainable, effective and affordable. For example modeling environmental polciies such as recycling machines and paper and conforming to specfic laws within a country. By using algebra and logic as a foundation to create models this address security flaws within organisations in relation to national laws, regulations and agreements.
